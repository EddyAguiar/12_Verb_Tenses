PRESENT TENSES
1. What is the Simple Present Tense?
✔️ Used for habits, general truths, and fixed arrangements.
🔹 Structure: Subject + V1 (base form)
⏳ Signal words: always, often, usually, every day
⚠️ For he/she/it, add -s/-es to the verb.
✓ I eat breakfast at 7 AM daily.
✓ The sun rises in the east.
2. What is the Present Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions happening now or temporary situations.
🔹 Structure: Subject + am/is/are + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: now, at the moment, currently
⚠️ Some verbs (like, want, know) are not used in continuous forms.
✓ She is studying for her exams now.
✓ They are playing football in the park.
3. What is the Present Perfect Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that happened at an unspecified time or have present results.
🔹 Structure: Subject + have/has + V3 (past participle)
⏳ Signal words: already, yet, ever, never, just
⚠️ Focuses on the result or experience, not when it happened.
✓ I have finished my homework.
✓ She has visited Paris three times.
4. What is the Present Perfect Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that started in the past and continue to the present (emphasizes duration).
🔹 Structure: Subject + have/has + been + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: for, since, all day/week
⚠️ Often used with action verbs (work, study, wait).
✓ He has been working here since 2010.
✓ They have been waiting for hours.
PAST TENSES
5. What is the Simple Past Tense?
✔️ Used for completed actions at a specific time in the past.
🔹 Structure: Subject + V2 (past form)
⏳ Signal words: yesterday, last week, in 2010, ago
⚠️ Regular verbs end in -ed, irregular verbs change form (go → went).
✓ I watched a movie last night.
✓ She went to Spain in 2019.
6. What is the Past Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that were in progress at a specific time in the past.
🔹 Structure: Subject + was/were + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: while, when, at 5 PM yesterday
⚠️ Often used for interrupted actions ("I was cooking when the phone rang").
✓ They were playing chess at 8 PM.
✓ She was studying when I called her.
7. What is the Past Perfect Tense?
✔️ Used for actions completed before another action in the past.
🔹 Structure: Subject + had + V3 (past participle)
⏳ Signal words: already, by the time, before
⚠️ Shows the order of past events ("After I had eaten, I left").
✓ She had finished her work before the meeting.
✓ They had already left when we arrived.
8. What is the Past Perfect Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that were ongoing before another past action (emphasizes duration).
🔹 Structure: Subject + had + been + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: for, since, how long
⚠️ Focuses on duration ("He had been working for 5 hours before the break").
✓ I had been waiting for 30 minutes when the bus came.
✓ They had been arguing before we intervened.
FUTURE TENSES
9. What is the Simple Future Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that will happen later.
🔹 Structure: Subject + will/shall + V1 (base form)
⏳ Signal words: tomorrow, next week, soon, in 2030
⚠️ "Shall" is formal and mostly used with I/we.
✓ She will call you tomorrow.
✓ We shall meet at the café.
10. What is the Future Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that will be in progress at a specific future time.
🔹 Structure: Subject + will be + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: at this time tomorrow, next Monday at 8 PM
⚠️ Emphasizes the duration of a future action.
✓ They will be traveling to Japan this time next month.
✓ I will be working at 9 AM tomorrow.
11. What is the Future Perfect Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that will be completed before a specific future time.
🔹 Structure: Subject + will have + V3 (past participle)
⏳ Signal words: by, by the time, before
⚠️ Focuses on completion before another future event.
✓ She will have finished her project by Friday.
✓ They will have left before we arrive.
12. What is the Future Perfect Continuous Tense?
✔️ Used for actions that will have been ongoing for a duration before a future time.
🔹 Structure: Subject + will have been + V1 + -ing
⏳ Signal words: for, since, by the time
⚠️ Emphasizes duration ("By 2025, I will have been working here for 10 years").
✓ He will have been sleeping for 8 hours by 7 AM.
✓ They will have been waiting for 2 hours when the train arrives.
